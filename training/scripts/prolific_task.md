# Window Measurement Photo Task

Thank you for participating! In this task, you will photograph a window in your home and measure it with a tape measure. This helps us build software that estimates window sizes from photos.

**Time:** 10-15 minutes
**Payment:** Listed on the Prolific task page

---

## What You Need

- A smartphone (any model made after 2018)
- A tape measure (inches)
- Access to a window you can measure

---

## Step 1: Take Photo 1 (Front View)

Stand **4 to 6 feet** directly in front of your window. Hold your phone in **landscape orientation** (sideways).

Take a single photo that includes:
- The **entire window frame** (all four edges visible)
- Some of the **surrounding wall** on all sides (at least a few inches)

**Do:**
- Keep the phone level (not tilted)
- Make sure the photo is in focus
- Include any curtains, blinds, or trim if present

**Do NOT:**
- Crop the photo after taking it
- Apply any filters or edits
- Use zoom (digital or optical)
- Stand closer than 4 feet or farther than 8 feet

---

## Step 2: Take Photo 2 (Angled View)

Move about **2-3 steps to the left or right** of where you took Photo 1. Take another photo of the **same window**, again in landscape orientation, with the full frame visible.

This gives us a second viewpoint of the same window, which improves measurement accuracy.

---

## Step 3: Measure the Window

Using your tape measure, measure the **inside of the window frame** (not the outer trim or casing):

1. **Width:** Measure from the inside-left edge to the inside-right edge, in inches.
2. **Height:** Measure from the inside-top edge to the inside-bottom edge, in inches.

Round to the nearest half inch (e.g., 36.5 inches is fine).

If you are unsure where the "inside frame" is: it is the opening where the glass sits, not the decorative molding around it.

---

## Step 4: Select Your Window Type

Choose the option that best describes your window:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Single-hung** | Bottom half slides up; top half is fixed |
| **Double-hung** | Both top and bottom halves slide up and down |
| **Casement** | Hinged on one side, swings outward with a crank |
| **Picture** | Fixed glass, does not open |
| **Sliding** | One or both panels slide left/right |
| **Bay/Bow** | Projects outward from the wall (3 or more panels) |
| **Arched** | Has a curved or rounded top |
| **Divided light** | Has grilles or muntins creating smaller panes |
| **Other** | None of the above |

If your window has both an arch AND divided lights, choose **Arched**. Pick the most prominent feature.

---

## Step 5: Upload and Submit

1. Upload **Photo 1** (front view)
2. Upload **Photo 2** (angled view)
3. Enter the **width** in inches
4. Enter the **height** in inches
5. Select the **window type** from the dropdown

Double-check your measurements before submitting. Common mistakes:
- Mixing up width and height
- Measuring the outer trim instead of the inner frame
- Entering centimeters instead of inches

---

## Common Questions

**Can I measure a door instead of a window?**
No, windows only.

**What if my window is very large (over 8 feet)?**
That is fine. Stand back far enough to capture the entire frame and measure as accurately as you can.

**What if I cannot reach the top of the window to measure height?**
Use a step stool, or measure as high as you can reach and estimate the remaining distance. Note this in the comments field if available.

**What if my window has multiple sections (like a bay window)?**
Measure the entire opening from one end to the other for width. Measure the height of the tallest section.

**My phone camera has multiple lenses. Which should I use?**
Use the default/main camera. Do not use ultrawide, telephoto, or macro modes.
